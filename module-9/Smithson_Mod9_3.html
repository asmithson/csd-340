Still, We Rise Each Morning

The world leans _________ some days-
like branches in winter, bare
but never broken.
We walk the cracked sidewalks,
coffee warm in our hands,
the sky holding back both sun and sorrow.

There is grief we do not name aloud,
quiet behind masks and headlines,
in the space between notifications.
Yet in the hush, something _________-
a child's laugh spilling across a room,
an old cat lounging in the sun.
Small testaments to the fact
that love does not vanish in _________.

We are not untouched, but we are not undone.
Hope lives in stubborn places:
in hands that build, in bread that rises,
in each morning, we choose again-
to speak gently, to make room,
to trust the soil enough
to _________ something new.

- A.S.
